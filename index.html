Style Guide — Technical Writing Guidelines

Guidelines for accurate, consistent, and user-focused documentation

Acceptable technical writing should be accurate, with a professional level of spelling, grammar, and punctuation. Good technical writing requires more than that.

I've collected the guidelines on this page over years of writing technical and instructional content. I consider every word I write carefully using these guidelines, striving to be:

  • Concise
  • Consistent
  • Clear
  • Complete
  • Empathetic
  • Pleasant to read
  • Adaptive

Be Concise

For every word, ask “Is it needed?”

Correct: Click Save.
Incorrect: You should now go ahead and click the Save button.

Be Consistent

Use the same terms, style, and formatting throughout.

Correct: Use either “sign in” everywhere.
Incorrect: Mixing “log in”, “login”, and “sign in”.

Be Clear

Avoid jargon, use plain language, and match the user’s vocabulary.

Correct: Select Print to print the document.
Incorrect: Execute the output routine to initiate hard-copy generation.

Be Complete

Ensure no step or explanation is missing.

Correct: After installation, restart your computer before opening the app.
Incorrect: After installation, open the app. (Restart required is missing)

Be Empathetic

Anticipate user confusion and provide reassurance.

Correct: If you forget your password, click Forgot Password to reset it safely.
Incorrect: Forgotten password? You’re stuck.

Be Pleasant to Read

Use active voice, positive phrasing, and a professional but approachable tone.

Correct: You can customize your dashboard by selecting Settings → Dashboard.
Incorrect: The dashboard is customizable if the appropriate option is selected in Settings.

Be Adaptive

Tailor the content to different audiences, platforms, and levels of expertise.

Correct: On mobile, tap the Menu icon. On desktop, click the Menu button.
Incorrect: Just click the Menu button. (No mention of mobile users)
