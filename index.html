Style Guide — Based on Microsoft Style Guide

Key points for consistent, user-focused documentation
  1. Use active voice whenever possible.
  2. Write in the second person (“you”) to address the reader directly.
  3. Keep sentences concise (average 15–20 words).
  4. Prefer short, simple words over complex ones.
  5. Use consistent terminology — define product names and features once and reuse them.
  6. Capitalize UI elements exactly as they appear in the interface.
  7. Use numbered lists for step-by-step instructions.
  8. Avoid jargon and acronyms unless they are industry standard.
  9. Provide context before action: explain what and why, then how.
  10. Use tables and visuals when they make content easier to scan.
  11. Always test procedures to ensure accuracy.
